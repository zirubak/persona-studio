How it works

Three steps, one afternoon.

From raw source material to a working roundtable in about twenty minutes — no fine-tuning, no training runs.
01 · INGEST

Feed it a voice.

Drop in essays, transcripts, a podcast feed, your Notion export, or a single URL. It builds a persona grounded in the actual writing — not a caricature.

$ persona ingest paulgraham.com/*.html
→ parsed 184 essays · 84k tokens
→ avatar "paul_graham" ready.
02 · ROUND

Pick a table.

Invite two to eight personas. Hand them a question, a pitch, a pull request. They'll disagree in character. You watch.

› simulate meeting with
@paul_graham @naval @dhh
about "should we raise a seed?"
03 · GRADE

Score the result.

Define what "good" looks like — clarity, rigor, kindness, whatever — and get a Ralph score against that rubric. Iterate until it lands.

Features

Built for the way you
actually think.

✻

Bring your own model

Anthropic, OpenAI, Ollama, llama.cpp. Swap providers per-persona. Run entirely local if you need to.

◷

Live round-table

Personas interrupt, agree, derail. Pace and voice are preserved. You can jump in at any turn.

◉

Ralph scoring

Define a rubric, watch responses graded against it in real time. Iterate until the answer clears your bar.

⟡

Private corpora

Your own notes, journals, Slack exports — build personas from anything. Nothing leaves your machine.

⌘

Keyboard first

⌘K for everything. Every action is scriptable from the CLI. Designed for people who live in terminals.

↯

JSON-first

Personas, simulations, results — all plain JSON. Git-friendly, diff-able, grep-able, yours.
